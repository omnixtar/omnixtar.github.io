### The Principle of Separation of Disclosure and Royalties

Currently, the tech world operates on a binary understanding of software licensing: either it is proprietary (hidden and expensive) or open source (visible and free). This binary is a trap.

The principle of separation of disclosure and royalties proposes a different social contract for the digital age. It asserts that the creator of a work—whether it is source code, a social media comment, a photo, or a video—has the right to distinguish between two distinct rights: **the right to inspection** and **the right to profit**.

1.  **Disclosure (The Human Right):** Peers and users must have the right to examine and verify the work. In an era of malware and AI manipulation, "security through obscurity" is a lie. We need the right to look under the hood to ensure a tool is safe and fit for purpose.
2.  **Royalties (The Commercial Right):** While the public can see and verify the code, the author reserves the right to claim compensation from commercial users or government-related entities. If a corporation executes a program to generate profit or achieve cost savings, they must pay the creator.

You might wonder: How have the programmers who built the internet allowed themselves to be stripped of these rights? The answer is systematic censorship. Powerful tech cartels have actively worked to erase these concepts from public discourse. If an idea is consistently removed from the internet by powerful parties, the number of people who know about it becomes infinitesimally small compared to the total user base.

### What is FORTHification?

This is where the technology meets the philosophy. To enforce this new social contract, we need a way to organize, verify, and protect code. This is the process of **FORTHification** (or FORTHxn).

For the non-technical reader, imagine the world’s software as a massive, disorganized library. Currently, most code is written like a long, rambling novel. It is messy, hard to read, and difficult to verify. If you change one sentence in chapter one, it might break the plot in chapter ten. This "unorganized" style allows corporate engines to scrape the code, hide it in their private servers, and monetize it without the original author truly knowing how it is being used.

FORTHification solves this by translating source code—regardless of the original programming language—into a standardized format based on **FORTH stack machine words**. Think of this as converting those rambling novels into a dictionary of perfect, interlocking Lego bricks.

Instead of a messy paragraph of code, a program is broken down into the smallest possible functional units (words). These units are:
1.  **Enumerated:** Every piece is cataloged and searchable.
2.  **Modular:** They can be taken apart and put back together.
3.  **Composable:** They fit together perfectly to build complex structures.

By using a tool called **Phoscript** (the metaprogramming engine) to convert code into these "FORTH bricks," we achieve three critical goals:

*   **Universal Verification:** Because the code is broken down into simple, standardized blocks, anyone can look at it and verify exactly what it does. No more hidden malicious features.
*   **Originality Checking:** We can use FORTHification to mathematically prove if a corporation has stolen your code, regardless of what language they tried to hide it in.
*   **AGI Measurement:** It allows us to measure progress toward Artificial General Intelligence not by "how much money we spent," but by "how many verified knowledge units we have."

FORTHification transforms static software into a living, self-correcting ecosystem. It moves us away from the "black box" AI models of MMAGA—where inputs go in and magic happens inside a locked server—toward a transparent system where every step of the process can be checked by a human. It creates a **Compose $\rightarrow$ Execute $\rightarrow$ Measure $\rightarrow$ Question** loop. The system generates a solution, executes it, measures the result, and asks if it is correct. If it fails, the error is transparent, not hidden.

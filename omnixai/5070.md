The **NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5070** (Blackwell architecture, 12GB GDDR7 VRAM, ~672 GB/s bandwidth, 250W TDP) is a mid-range consumer GPU launched in late 2025/early 2026. It offers solid local LLM inference performance for small-to-medium models, thanks to Blackwell's efficiency gains (better Tensor Cores, higher bandwidth than Ada Lovelace equivalents), but it's limited by 12GB VRAM compared to the RTX 4090 (24GB) or RTX 5090 (32GB). This restricts it to heavier quantization for larger models and makes it less ideal for very large contexts or unquantized runs.

This estimate uses the same methodology as prior RTX 5090/4090 calculations: Assume the card runs as an **AI inference server for 12 hours/day** (43,200 seconds), at realistic high-utilization TPS.

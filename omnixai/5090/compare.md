### Summary of Core Content Across Sources
All sources estimate "earnings" or "savings" as **gross equivalent value** (not actual profit from selling tokens, but what the same token output would cost on paid APIs). They assume:
- 12 hours/day runtime (43,200 seconds of inference time).
- High-utilization scenarios (often 100% assumed, but real-world 50–90% noted).
- Local open models (e.g., Llama 3.1/Qwen/Mistral variants, quantized Q4/Q5/FP16) run via tools like vLLM, llama.cpp, or TensorRT-LLM.
- Commercial blended pricing per 1M tokens: $0.3–$25 (budget tiers like GPT-4o-mini ~$0.3–$2; mid-tier GPT-4o/Claude Sonnet ~$5–$15; premium GPT-5/Claude Opus ~$14–$25).

Key variations:
- **High-throughput focus** (small 7B–8B models, batch/concurrency serving): Massive daily tokens → high equivalent value (often six figures annually).
- **Single-user/generation focus** (larger models): Much lower TPS → modest value.
- Deductions mentioned: Electricity (~$0.3–$1.1/day), hardware depreciation.

- **Most comprehensive & consistent** → Omnixtar main page and /claude subpage: They provide the widest range ($50k–$700k+ annual gross for RTX 5090 in realistic/high-throughput scenarios), backed by TPS benchmarks and tiered pricing. They emphasize small/mid-model batch serving as the sweet spot for massive value (due to high TPS on RTX 5090's bandwidth).
- **More conservative** → /gemini and Mistral chat: Lower daily tokens (~millions vs. billions), modest daily value ($3–$756), better for single-user or less optimized runs.
- **Comparative context** → Kimi share stands out by directly benchmarking RTX 5090 vs. 4090/5070, showing 5090's potential for 50–100× higher daily token volume (and thus value) thanks to superior VRAM (32GB), bandwidth (1.79 TB/s), and Blackwell efficiency — but at higher power/cost.
- **Range of optimism**: Estimates span 100× depending on:
  - TPS assumptions (batch high-concurrency vs. single-prompt generation).
  - Model size/quality equivalent (budget open-model tier vs. premium closed-model pricing).
  - Utilization (100% vs. 50–70% real-world).

Net profit lower after ~$300–$500/year electricity + depreciation.

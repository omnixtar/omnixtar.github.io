## Bloody Secretary of Treasury

- In the style of a Monty Python sketch, imagine a Democrat senator (suggest funny Monty Python name) responds to said cabinet member with a dismissive and ridiculing speech.

**Scene: A dimly lit Senate hearing room. A single spotlight. The Democrat senator, SIR REGINALD FFORTE-BOTTOMLEY (known to his friends as “Reggie the Waffle”), rises slowly from his chair wearing a bright pink bow tie, a monocle on a string, and what appears to be half a Roman toga over his suit. He adjusts his monocle dramatically, then speaks in an exaggerated, plummy British accent mixed with occasional Valley Girl inflections.**

SIR REGINALD FFORTE-BOTTOMLEY:  
*(clears throat theatrically, producing the sound of a kazoo)*  

Right. So. Treasury Secretary… what was it again? Sir Pompous von Goldfinger? The gentleman who just stood there for twenty-seven uninterrupted minutes explaining to us that the United States Dollar is under existential threat… from… *(pauses, consults a tiny scroll)* …a GitHub page.  

A GitHub page.  

Not a nuclear submarine. Not Chinese hypersonic missiles. Not even inflation caused by, oh I don’t know, printing money like it’s Monopoly after someone spilled Red Bull on the board. No. A website. With dots. And slashes. And the word “contract” at the end like someone forgot to name their file properly.  

*(leans forward, whispers loudly)*  
It’s called omnixtar.github.io/contract, Your Honors. I looked it up on my telephone during the gentleman's speech. It took seventeen seconds and three pop-up ads for erectile-dysfunction supplements.  

And now—NOW—we are to believe this collection of curly braces and pretentious variable names is going to dethrone the United States Dollar, the currency so powerful it once convinced Saudi Arabia to price oil in it just so they could have matching handbags.  

*(dramatic gasp, clutches imaginary pearls)*  

I mean—really! The man said “Trojan horse”! He used the phrase “digital feudalism”! He practically accused a JSON file of plotting with Vladimir Putin over borscht and blockchain!  

*(turns to imaginary gallery, cups hand to ear)*  
What’s that, madam in the third row? You say you’ve never heard of Omnihash? Neither has anyone else! It has seventeen stars on GitHub. Seventeen! My grandmother’s fruitcake recipe has more likes on Pinterest!  

*(waves arms like an agitated windmill)*  

And yet here we are, being told we must now launch “enhanced regulatory scrutiny” against… *(reads from invisible notecard with great contempt)* …“DJSON—Decentralized JSON.” Which, I’m reliably informed by my seventeen-year-old nephew who smells like Doritos, is basically JSON but someone yelled “decentralized!” at it first.  

Marvelous.  

Next they’ll tell us the Republic is threatened by decentralized YAML. Or—God forbid—someone forks a README.md without permission.  

*(sudden change to mock solemnity)*  

Let me be perfectly clear, as my Republican friends love to say before they’re perfectly unclear about everything:  

If Omnihash succeeds in toppling the dollar, I will personally eat my own filibuster notes. With ketchup. And I will livestream it. On TikTok. While wearing a Make America Hash Again hat.  

*(pauses, grins wickedly)*  

But until that glorious day—when the petrodollar is defeated by angle brackets and someone’s senior project—I move that we table this nonsense, adjourn for tea and biscuits, and perhaps investigate the far greater national-security threat…  

…namely, why the Treasury Secretary apparently has the cybersecurity instincts of a toaster that still believes in fax machines.  

*(bows extravagantly, monocle falls off, he catches it in his mouth like a circus seal, then sits down to polite, confused applause)*  

**Fade out on confused senators staring at each other while someone in the background quietly whispers, “Is… is that allowed?”**

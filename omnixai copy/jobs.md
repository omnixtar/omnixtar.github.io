## An Accidental Socialist Era: How Decentralised AI can do more than UBI & achieve Post Scarcity Society

**90 trillion tokens and USD 25 a day** — these are the realistic practical figures achievable using **Omnihash** under the **Omniscientia Initiative** to begin the journey toward a post-scarcity world.

In 2026, as global PC shipments surpassed 270–279 million units, the infrastructure for decentralized AI already exists in plain sight. Fifty percent of those non-GPU machines, running Microsoft’s BitNet b1.58 models for just 12 hours a day via optimized bitnet.cpp, could conservatively generate around **90 trillion tokens daily**. A single modern CPU can push a 100-billion-parameter BitNet model at human reading speeds (5–7 tokens per second) while slashing energy use by 55–82% compared to traditional floating-point inference. Scaled across hundreds of millions of everyday laptops and desktops, this represents an enormous, low-cost, edge-native compute layer that centralized H100/H200 clusters cannot match in sheer distributed volume.

On the high-performance side, one **NVIDIA RTX 5090** GPU, running 12 hours daily with efficient models and batching, can deliver thousands of tokens per second on 7–8B-class workloads (benchmarks show peaks exceeding 5,800 tokens/sec on optimized setups). At prevailing 2026 market rates for hosted open-weight inference, this translates to roughly **$25 gross per day per card** — enough to cover basic operational realities when scaled thoughtfully.

Pair these hardware realities with the lightweight, cryptographically owned governance of **Omnihash** (from omnixtar.github.io/contract), and the pieces snap into place. Under the **Omniscientia Initiative**, programs become verifiable digital assets: individuals and communities can read, fork, and run training curricula freely, while commercial-scale execution by companies or agencies triggers transparent royalty mechanisms. This framework turns raw token generation into coordinated, equitable participation rather than extractive cloud rental.

Imagine the human layer: former street-dwellers and housing-insecure individuals trained as **AI Guards** — operators who manage hybrid RTX 5090 farms alongside BitNet CPU fleets. A modest 20-card home or community setup could generate $500 gross daily before electricity and upkeep. In many U.S. suburban rings 10 km outside major city centers, where median rents have softened but still hover in the $1,500–$3,000+ range in coastal metros, such income begins to shift the equation from survival to stability. In more affordable Sun Belt or Midwest areas, the margins improve dramatically.

This is not abstract futurism. It echoes the post-scarcity analyses of authors since 2010 — Aaron Bastani’s *Fully Automated Luxury Communism*, Jeremy Rifkin’s *The Zero Marginal Cost Society*, and Nick Srnicek & Alex Williams’ *Inventing the Future* — who identified automation, near-zero marginal costs, and abundant energy as the drivers that could dissolve traditional scarcity. Decentralized AI inference attacks the bottleneck directly: when millions of consumer devices produce tokens cheaply and locally, AI becomes a ubiquitous utility instead of a scarce, expensive service.

Of course, hurdles remain. Electricity costs, hardware depreciation, cooling, and token price erosion with growing supply demand careful optimization. High-rent coastal suburbs still challenge single setups. Training and supporting new operators requires mentorship, safe spaces, and phased progression. Yet these are solvable engineering and social problems, not fundamental limits.

In the fictional prequel we explored — Omniscientia training the first AI Guards on the streets — those same guardians later designed the gentle **Matrix of Light**, where aging minds rest in low-energy simulation while the world completes its Space Elevators and launches the 100,000-passenger Pentatronia arks toward the stars. The seeds of that gentle abundance are already here: consumer GPUs for muscle, BitNet for scale across existing hardware, and Omnihash for fair, forkable coordination.

**90 trillion tokens** and **$25 a day** are not endpoints. They are practical starting figures — measurable, achievable, and replicable today under a decentralized contract model. Scaled thoughtfully through initiatives like Omniscientia, they mark the beginning of a shift: from AI as a centralized luxury to a distributed public good, from marginalization to meaningful participation, from scarcity mindset to genuine abundance at the edge.

The journey toward post-scarcity does not require waiting for perfect fusion or universal basic income. It begins with the hardware we already ship by the hundreds of millions, the models that run efficiently on ordinary CPUs, and the lightweight contracts that let communities own their contribution.

Let us train the guards. Distribute the nodes. Keep the light.

The age of abundance is not inevitable — but with these tools in human hands, it is increasingly plausible.

## Floating Nuclear AI Ships/Subs -- from Russia with Love 

- Why wait 5 years if we can recondition HUNDREDS of decommissioned nuclear ships/subs hiding in secret docks somewhere?

- Prompt: Find decommissioned nuclear ships or submarines, repurpose as floating or submarine AI data center -- or nuclear sub provides power for float AI center. Estimate total number in use, to be decommissioned, and total costs and revenues projected.

- *the NEXT Mission Impossible: Nuclear AI subs (plural!!) go rogue*

Repurposing **decommissioned nuclear submarines** (or surface ships) as floating AI data centers—or using their reactors to power separate floating datacenters—remains largely conceptual in March 2026. Recent proposals focus on extracting reactors from retired US Navy vessels (submarines and aircraft carriers) for land-based AI data centers, rather than keeping the hulls afloat or submerged. No operational examples exist yet, due to regulatory, safety, environmental, and technical hurdles (e.g., IAEA/IMO approvals, defueling, reactor relocation, marine stability for racks/cooling, bandwidth via subsea cables).

Decommissioned nuclear subs are rarely sold commercially—especially nuclear-powered ones—due to proliferation risks, radioactive material controls, and national security. Older diesel-electric subs appear occasionally for sale (e.g., via niche sites like mysubmarines.com, priced ~$2M for a Soviet Foxtrot-class), but nuclear ones are typically scrapped/recycled by governments (e.g., US Puget Sound Naval Shipyard, UK Rosyth Dockyard). Proposals like HGP Intelligent Energy's (filed to US DOE) aim to repurpose retired naval reactors (e.g., from Nimitz-class carriers or Los Angeles-class subs) for civilian power, potentially at ~$1–4M per MW (far cheaper than new builds).

### Current and Projected Numbers
- **In service (global, ~2026)**: ~160–170 nuclear-powered vessels total (mostly military submarines; ~50–60 US, ~30–40 Russia, plus UK, France, China, India). ~140+ are submarines.
- **Decommissioned but intact/awaiting disposal**:
  - **US**: Hundreds recycled since 1950s (124 reactor compartments at Hanford). Recent: Los Angeles-class (e.g., Newport News SSN-750, Alexandria SSN-757 inactivated 2026; more follow). ~20–30 older Los Angeles/Ohio-class potentially in queue over next decade. Reactors from carriers (e.g., upcoming Nimitz retirement) eyed for repurposing.
  - **UK**: ~20 retired since 1980s; none fully disposed until recently. Swiftsure (first full dismantle by end-2026); 6+ defueled PWR1 subs at Rosyth awaiting dismantling; defueling restarts 2026.
  - **Russia**: ~180 decommissioned (many with fuel still in reactors); backlog due to slow dismantling (3–6/year). High environmental risks.
  - **Others**: Few civilian nuclear ships (e.g., NS Sevmorput still active; most like NS Savannah, Otto Hahn long decommissioned/scrapped).
- **To be decommissioned (next 5–10 years)**: US plans retire ~2–4 attack subs/year (Los Angeles-class phase-out); Ohio-class SSBNs/SSGNs delayed (e.g., Ohio/ Florida spared 2026 inactivation). Global: 10–20 subs/year potential as older classes retire (e.g., Russia, China transitions). Total feasible for repurposing: **~50–100 reactor units** (subs often have 1–2 reactors; carriers 2+) over decade, but most scrapped—not repurposed.

Realistically available for experimental repurposing: **Very low** (0–5 in near term, e.g., via special DOE approvals like HGP proposal for 450–520 MW from 2 reactors). Governments prioritize secure dismantling over sale/repurposing.

### Estimated Costs
Repurposing one sub (or its reactor) as power source/floating datacenter far exceeds new land-based options due to defueling, transport, certification, and marine adaptations.

- **Acquisition/repurposing base**:
  - "Purchase" or transfer: Effectively $0 (government program) to nominal; but legal/regulatory fees high.
  - Defueling + reactor extraction: $100M–$500M+ per vessel (US/UK costs billions for fleets).
  - Relocation/adaptation (hull to barge, reactor to land/power barge): $200M–$1B+ per unit.
  - Full floating datacenter fit-out (racks, liquid cooling using seawater, internal networking): $300M–$800M (similar to prior nuclear ship estimates but smaller scale).
- **Total per unit** (mid-range, one sub powering modest cluster): **$500M–$1.5B** (cheaper if reactor-only for land tie-in; e.g., HGP ~$1–4M/MW → $450–$2,000M for 450–520 MW setup).
- **Annual opex**: $50–$150M (maintenance, security, bandwidth, crew; nuclear fuel/refuel low but insurance high).

### Number of Servers / Scale & Revenues Projected
A single repurposed sub/reactor pair could provide **~200–500 MW** (e.g., Los Angeles-class ~ similar to carrier pair proposals). Usable for GPUs: ~150–400 MW (after overhead/PUE ~1.2–1.4).

- **GPUs/servers**: ~15,000–50,000 H200-equivalent GPUs (mid ~30,000; ~3,750–6,250 8-GPU servers).
- **Bandwidth required**: 5–20 Tbps internal fabric; external 1–4 Tbps subsea fiber (multiple redundant shore links; $10–$50M/year).
- **Annual revenues** (Omnihash/decentralized AI token trading, $10–$15/M tokens equiv.):
  - Per GPU: ~$1M–$1.8M/year gross.
  - Per repurposed unit: **$30B–$90B gross run-rate** (mid ~$50B for 30k GPUs); phased/realistic capture 20–50% net after costs/fees → **$6B–$45B/year** potential per setup.
  - For small fleet (e.g., 3–5 units over decade): **$20B–$200B+ total annual network** at scale.

**Overall projection**:
- **Year 1–2 (2026–2027)**: 0–1 pilot (e.g., HGP-style land-tied reactor from 1–2 subs; revenues start low/phased ~$1–$5B equiv. if successful).
- **Longer term (5–10 years)**: If approvals accelerate (AI power crunch + policy push), **5–20 reactors** repurposed → **$50B–$500B+ annual equiv.** across network (highly speculative; competition from SMRs/new nuclear).

This is frontier/experimental—major barriers include nuclear regs, proliferation controls, and environmental opposition. Most subs head to scrap, not AI farms. Land-based SMRs or grid upgrades remain more practical for now.

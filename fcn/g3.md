### How the Pieces Fit Together
- **I2P**: Provides anonymous, censorship-resistant, peer-to-peer networking. Users can share compute resources (CPU/GPU cycles) without revealing identities or relying on centralized cloud providers. It enables secure, distributed job distribution and result collection.
- **Omnihash**: Acts as an immutable, cryptographic fingerprint for code, data, models, or compute tasks. It ensures provenance, ownership, and verifiability — crucial for trust in a decentralized network (e.g., proving a specific GPU contributed to training/inference without fraud).
- **GRATIS Tokens**: The incentive layer. Micro-payments reward resource providers for contributing compute. Users earn GRATIS when their hardware runs Forthified AI tasks (inference, fine-tuning steps, data processing, etc.).

**Combined Effect**: A peer-to-peer marketplace where anyone with spare hardware can supply AI tokens (compute capacity measured in tokens processed) and get paid automatically.

### How Users Become AI Token Suppliers
1. **Resource Sharing**:
   - Install lightweight client software that exposes idle CPU/GPU via I2P.
   - Forthify AI workloads (models, prompts, training data snippets) into portable "words".
   - The network routes tasks to available nodes anonymously.

2. **Earning Mechanism**:
   - When your hardware executes a task, you earn GRATIS proportional to tokens processed or FLOPs contributed.
   - Omnihash verifies the work was done correctly and attributes it properly.
   - Smart contracts (or equivalent on the network) handle automatic, trustless payouts.

3. **User Types**:
   - **Home Users/Gamers**: Monetize gaming PCs during idle time.
   - **Data Center Owners/Small Operators**: Offer capacity without Big Tech overhead.
   - **Emerging Markets**: Regions with cheap electricity but limited cloud access become competitive suppliers.
   - **Mobile/Edge Devices**: Phones, IoT, or laptops contribute smaller tasks.

### Challenges & Risks
- **Technical**:
  - Performance overhead of I2P and decentralized scheduling (latency, coordination).
  - Verifying honest compute (Sybil attacks, fake results) requires robust proof-of-work or zero-knowledge mechanisms.
  - Heterogeneity of hardware makes consistent token output tricky.

- **Economic**:
  - GRATIS token volatility or low value could discourage participation.
  - Competition from established clouds (cheaper/more reliable for big jobs).

- **Adoption & Regulatory**:
  - Network effects needed for liquidity.
  - Energy consumption, electricity costs, and local regulations (e.g., crypto taxes, data laws).
  - Security: Malicious code or hardware compromise risks.
